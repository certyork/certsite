Mission Statement: We listen to people with experience of mental health issues, to influence service provision through research and evaluation.

Our core values


Insight

Our lived experience of mental health issues means that we have insight into the lives and experiences of other people with these difficulties. We use this insight in all stages of the evaluation process to produce more valid and useful findings.

Empowering people

Our priority is to empower people who may struggle to participate in traditional research and evaluation to get fully involved in the whole research and evaluation process, and to ensure that their voices are always heard.

Alongside

We work alongside the users of the service we are evaluating, co-producing the research as much as is practical. This can include training them in the research methods we are using, for example, how to create and carry out interviews. This means they have the skills and confidence to fully engage with the whole research process alongside CERT researchers.

Creativity

We believe using and developing creative research methods, especially those which make the experience less daunting for participants, for example, photographs, music and creative writing. The use of non-traditional and creative research methods makes participating more accessible for people who struggle with traditional methods, for example, because it is more interesting to them and this often reduces the demand placed on them.

Accessibility

By empowering people and working alongside them, our goal is to remove the power dynamic which often exists in traditional research. We also ensure the dissemination of findings is accessible to different audiences, for example, creating formal written reports, easy-read reports, film and theatre performances. We also seek to make CERT fully inclusive and accessible to all people who are interested in joining us. On-going training and development allows people to fully participate.
